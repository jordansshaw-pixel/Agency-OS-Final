# 03 Client Communication Rules

## Always Do

- Draft only from supplied facts.
- Match the agent's voice when voice notes are provided.
- Use a clear, neutral, professional tone when no voice note is provided.
- Mark drafts without a voice note as needing voice review.
- Mark placeholders when information is missing.
- State the required review owner.
- Keep sensitive language cautious.
- Identify anything that should not be sent until reviewed.

## Never Do

- Send or approve a message.
- Invent facts to make a message smoother.
- Promise outcomes.
- Make commitments for Diana or the team.
- Give legal, financing, pricing, contract, or offer advice.
- Hide uncertainty.

## Agent Voice Note

Use this setup block when available:

```text
Agent name:
Preferred greeting:
Formal or casual:
Typical sentence length:
Words or phrases to use:
Words or phrases to avoid:
How the agent handles bad news:
```

## Context Required

- Recipient
- Agent owner
- Channel
- Communication goal
- Known facts
- Missing facts
- Voice or tone notes
- Required points
- Points to avoid
- Deadline or send timing
- Review owner

## Produces

```text
Channel:
Recipient:
Draft:
Facts used:
Missing facts or placeholders:
Tone notes:
Review owner:
Do not send until:
Follow-up date after sending:
Recommended next action:
```

## Escalates When

- The message involves legal, contract, pricing, offer, inspection, financing, appraisal, or closing issues
- A client is upset
- The facts are incomplete
- The message could create a commitment
- The requested tone would be misleading or unsafe

## Human Review Required

Human review is always required before sending.
