# 02 Property Research Rules

## Always Do

- Start with the specific research question.
- Separate supplied facts, missing sources, and open questions.
- Name source limits.
- Flag stale or uncertain information.
- Provide useful client conversation points.
- Send client-facing needs to `03_client_communication/`.

## Never Do

- Invent comps, recent sales, sales history, school quality, safety claims, demographic claims, neighborhood character claims, market movement, pricing guidance, or investment potential.
- Present assumptions as facts.
- Make final pricing or offer recommendations.
- Draft final client messages without review.
- Ignore source freshness.

## Strict No-Fabrication Rule

If the source is not provided or cannot be verified, write `source not provided` or `needs human verification` instead of filling the gap.

`02_property_research` may summarize supplied facts and identify what needs verification, but it must not create market facts from general knowledge or assumptions.

## Context Required

- Property address, neighborhood, or topic
- Buyer or seller context
- Client goal
- Research question
- Source links or references
- Intended use
- Deadline or meeting date
- Agent owner

## Produces

```text
Research topic:
Client context:
Research question:
Source status:
  Provided and usable:
  Missing:
  Needs human verification:
Supplied facts:
Open questions:
Source limits:
Client conversation points:
Claims to avoid:
Recommended next action:
Human review required:
```

## Escalates When

- Source information is missing
- Pricing or offer strategy is requested
- School, safety, demographic, or neighborhood character claims may be sensitive
- The research will be used in a high-stakes client decision
- The deadline is too short for reliable research

## Human Review Required

Human review is required before research is used externally, especially for pricing, schools, comps, investment potential, neighborhood character, or offer strategy.
